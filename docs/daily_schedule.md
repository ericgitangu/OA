## Zazen Instructions

Zazen (seated meditation) is the foundation of the monk mode practice. It clears mental noise before deep work begins.

### Setup
1. Sit on a cushion or chair with spine straight, chin slightly tucked
2. Hands in cosmic mudra: left hand on right, palms up, thumbs lightly touching
3. Eyes half-open, gaze resting 2-3 feet ahead on the floor
4. Mouth closed, tongue resting on the roof of the mouth

### Practice
1. Breathe naturally through the nose
2. Count exhales from 1 to 10, then restart
3. When thoughts arise, acknowledge them without engagement and return to counting
4. If you lose count, start again from 1 — this is the practice, not a failure

### Duration
- Start with 15 minutes (3:30-3:45 AM)
- The timer is not optional — sit for the full duration even when the mind resists
- Quality comes from consistency, not duration

### Why This Matters for Code
- Pattern recognition improves when the mind is quiet
- Debugging requires sustained attention without frustration
- The practice of returning to breath mirrors returning to the problem after distraction
- "Beginner's mind" (shoshin) prevents you from forcing familiar solutions onto novel problems
